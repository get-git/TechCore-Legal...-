 In order to deliver value to the customer, the EXO Mask will feature a two-part solution. The physical device will host high-grade filters and a variety of sensors that collect and transmit relevant data.

In addition, a software application will receive said data transfer and display health indicators for the user on a mobile device.   The EXO Mask is a wearable, multi-modality mask device for filtering contaminates from the air while providing users with environmental and health information by measuring temperature, respiratory rate, respiratory volume, motion, and air quality.

The data is transferred wirelessly through connection to a Bluetooth device or the internet. In the present invention, various sensors and technologies are integrated into the mask to enable the user with full functionality in one unit. Sensors are implemented to provide wearers with actionable data collection that can be utilized for health monitoring and assessment.

LIVE DEMO

The EXO mask solution hosts high-grade, replaceable filtration as well as a sensor array that collects and transmits biodata. The physical device is accompanied by a smartphone application that will receive data transmissions via Bluetooth and display health indicators for the user at their convenience. The device is capable of sensing both environmental and health parameters through the measurement of temperature, respiratory rate and volume, motion, and air quality.

Among the embedded sensors include a temperature gauge located in the strap, an airflow sensor in the breathing channel to monitor respiratory health, and an accelerometer to detect motion. The accumulated sensor data then runs through algorithms in the software to inform the user in real time about their health status. For the technology to function properly, a sealed fit is required. This presents the opportunity to inform the user if the device fit ever becomes compromised, because it would directly correlate with unreasonably skewed sensor data.

Over time, the device will establish a baseline for the user based on lifetime usage. Similar to a machine learning system, the device and its software counterpart will grow increasingly accurate and effective for the wearer. This will enable the device to filter out non-relevant data more effectively and keep the wearer safe and healthy.
